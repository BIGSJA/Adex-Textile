ABOUT US


Adex Textiles is an integrated textile producer with an existing textile mill. Adex Textiles began a complete modernization to make it a state of the art manufacturing facility able to compete in today's global marketplace. The upgrades have been a continuing, ongoing process with the installation of the latest generation in weaving technology. The company has survived decades of turmoil in the textile industry due to a combination of modernization, diversity of product and customer mix, targeted marketing and the willingness of our associates to adapt to changing conditions.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS



Both textile and fabric are general terms for a fabric used to make clothing. A textile is a form of fabric that is made from fiber. The main difference between fabric and textile is the way in which it is made. Fabric is a material made from natural materials or synthetic materials. Textile is a general term for a group of fibers made by spinning, weaving, knitting, crocheting, felting, or braiding.

Examples of textiles are linen, cotton, wool, nylon, silk, polyester, acrylics, and blends like polyester/cotton. These are the different types of textiles.

It is called textile because the term textile is an adjective that means “made of threads”. A textile is a manufactured material made from interlacing fibers.

Textile waste is a broad category of waste from the textile industry, which includes discarded fabric, yarn, and clothing. The largest amount of waste produced by the global textile industry is in the form of discarded fabric or “cuttings”. The second-largest amount of waste is in the form of yarn. Additionally, textile waste can also encompass clothing, rags, and leather. The third amount of textile waste is a form of industrial and domestic waste that refers to the products of the textile industry that is not recycled.

Yes, paper is a textile, a thin, flat material made from plant or synthetic fibers, used to make clothing and other products.
